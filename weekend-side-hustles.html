Weekend Side Hustles: How to Make an Extra $200 in 2 Days

July 20, 2026 · 6 min read · by MoneyWise
Don't have time for a whole new job? Here's what's realistically achievable in a single weekend — no long onboarding, no big investment.

Saturday Morning: Declutter and List

Spend 90 minutes photographing and listing 5–10 items you no longer need. Local pickup items can sell same-day. See our guide on what to flip from around your house for the fastest-selling categories.

Saturday Afternoon: Task-Based Gig Work

🔧 Same-Day Gigs

Apps for furniture assembly, moving help, yard work, or errands typically pay same-day or next-day. Two or three short gigs can realistically add up to $60–100 depending on your area and rates.

Saturday Evening: Food Delivery During Peak Hours

If you have a car or bike, weekend dinner rush (roughly 5–8pm) is typically the highest-earning window for delivery apps due to demand-based pay boosts.

Sunday: Bundle and Sell What Didn't Move

Combine slower-moving items into a "bundle deal" — buyers are often more willing to pay for convenience than individually priced items.

Sunday Afternoon: Local Services

Depending on the season: car washing, pressure washing, pet sitting, or dog walking are all bookable same-day through local apps or community groups. See our full guide on starting a pressure washing business if this becomes a repeat weekend earner.

Realistic Expectations

$200 in a weekend is achievable but depends heavily on your local market, what you have to sell, and how much time you actually put in. Treat the low end of any income estimate as the realistic baseline, and anything above that as a bonus.

Why This Works Better Than a New "Side Business"

Weekend hustles have no onboarding, no inventory investment, and no long-term commitment — you can try one this weekend and simply not repeat it if it's not worth your time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is $200 in a weekend realistic for beginners?

It's achievable but not guaranteed — it depends on your local gig market and how many items you have to sell. Many people land in the $80–150 range on their first attempt and improve from there.

Which weekend hustle pays the most per hour?

Task-based gig work and local services (pressure washing, car detailing) tend to pay more per hour than selling items, but selling items requires no physical labor.
