Why Isn't My Stuff Selling? How to Sell Faster on Facebook Marketplace

By MoneyWise · Last updated July 22, 2026 · 6 min read

You listed it a week ago. Forty views, two saves, zero messages — or worse, five "Is this available?" ghosts. Meanwhile someone else sold the same thing in a day. The difference is almost never luck. Stuck listings have fixable problems, and they're nearly always one of the five below.

Fix #1: Your First Photo Is Killing You

Marketplace is a scrolling feed. Buyers see one photo, one line, one price — and decide in half a second. Your first photo needs to be bright, uncluttered, and taken from a slight angle (not dead-on). Move the item near a window, clear everything else out of frame, and wipe it down first. Retaking one photo has revived more dead listings than any other fix.

  • Shoot in daylight, never with the flash at night
  • Fill the frame with the item — no messy garage background
  • Add 5–8 photos total: all sides, brand labels, flaws included (honesty pre-answers questions)

Fix #2: Your Price Isn't Wrong — It's Uncompetitive

Before pricing anything, search Marketplace for your exact item and sort by nearest. Whatever the three cheapest comparable listings are, that's the real market — not what you paid, and not what it's "worth." Price 10% under the cheapest comparable and it sells first. If you're not in a hurry, price at market and wait it out.

The $95 trick: Marketplace search filters use round numbers. A dresser at $95 shows up in "under $100" searches; the same dresser at $100 often gets filtered out. Price just under the round number.

Fix #3: Your Title Doesn't Match What Buyers Type

Buyers search "solid wood dresser," "sectional couch," "girls bike 20 inch." If your title says "Beautiful vintage piece — must see!" nobody will ever find it. Write titles like a search query: brand + item + key detail. "West Elm Mid-Century Dresser – 6 Drawer Walnut" beats "Gorgeous dresser!!" every single time.

Fix #4: You're Listing at Dead Hours

Listings get their biggest visibility boost in the first few hours. List Thursday or Friday evening (6–9pm) when buyers are planning weekend pickups, or Sunday evening. A great listing posted Monday at 9am spends its visibility window while everyone's at work.

Fix #5: You're Responding Wrong (Or Too Slow)

Answer within minutes when possible — Marketplace boosts sellers with fast response rates, and buyers message three sellers at once. First reply wins the sale. And when a lowballer offers half price, counter instead of ignoring: "Can't do $40, but $70 today and it's yours." A surprising number accept.

Stay safe: Meet in daylight at a public spot when possible (many police stations have designated exchange zones), take cash or Zelle/Venmo confirmed in person, and never accept overpayment "by accident" — that's always a scam. For expensive items, bring a friend to pickup.

The Relist Reset

If a listing has sat for two weeks: don't renew it — delete it and create a fresh listing with your improved photos, title, and price. Fresh listings re-enter the "new" feed where most browsing happens. Renewed listings stay buried. This one move, combined with a better first photo, un-sticks the majority of dead listings.

Once things start moving, Marketplace stops being a decluttering tool and starts being an income stream — buying underpriced items and reselling them is one of the most accessible flips there is. Frequently Asked Questions

Why do people say "Is this available?" and disappear?

It's Marketplace's one-tap auto-message — treat it as a doorbell, not a conversation. Reply instantly with "Yes! When would you like to pick it up?" to force a real answer. Expect maybe 1 in 4 to respond; that's normal.

Should I offer delivery?

Offering delivery for a fee ($20–40 within 15 minutes' drive) noticeably widens your buyer pool for furniture — many interested buyers simply don't have a truck. Mention it in the listing.

Is it worth listing on multiple platforms?

Yes — cross-post to OfferUp and Craigslist for local sales, and eBay for shippable items. It's free exposure. Just remember to take listings down everywhere once something sells.
