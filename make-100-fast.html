How to Make $100 Fast (Real Ways That Actually Work)

By MoneyWise · July 6, 2026 · 7 min read
Updated: July 2026

Sometimes you just need $100. Maybe rent is due, your car needs a repair, or you just want some extra cash for the weekend. Whatever the reason, $100 is actually pretty easy to get if you know where to look — and most of the ways on this list can pay out within a day or two.

Here's what works. No surveys, no "unlimited earning potential" nonsense, no referral schemes. Just real stuff.

1. Sell Stuff You Already Own

Facebook Marketplace / OfferUp

Time to cash
Same day
Effort
Low
Potential
$50–$300+

Walk around your house and look at everything you haven't touched in 6 months. Old electronics, clothes, furniture, exercise equipment, tools — people buy all of it. Take decent photos, price it 20% below what similar items sell for, and you can have money in hand the same day someone picks it up.

Most people are sitting on $200–$500 worth of stuff they don't even think about. Old gaming controllers, a jacket you never wear, a blender still in the box — all sellable.

Quick tip: Electronics, power tools, and anything with a brand name sell the fastest. Avoid listing random junk — focus on items someone would actually search for.

2. Do a Quick Local Service

Lawn Mowing / Yard Work

Per job
$30–$80
Time to cash
Same day
Startup cost
$0 (borrow equipment)

Knock on two or three doors in your neighborhood and offer to mow lawns. Most people will pay $30–$50 for a standard yard. Two jobs and you've hit $100. You don't need your own equipment — ask a neighbor or family member if you can borrow a mower.

Pressure Washing

Per job
$75–$150
Time to cash
Same day
Startup cost
$0 (rent equipment)

One driveway or patio job can clear $75–$150. You can rent a pressure washer from Home Depot for about $40 for 4 hours, do a job, and still pocket $50–$100 after costs. Most homeowners will pay cash on the spot.

3. Gig Apps (Same-Day Pay)

DoorDash / Uber Eats / Instacart

Hourly
$12–$20
Time to cash
Same day (Fast Pay)
Signup time
1–2 days

If you have a car, delivering food or groceries is one of the most reliable ways to hit $100 in a single day. DoorDash and Uber Eats both have Fast Pay options where you can cash out instantly after completing deliveries. Work a busy dinner shift (5pm–9pm) and you can realistically make $60–$100 in one session.

TaskRabbit

Hourly
$20–$60+
Jobs
Moving, assembly, cleaning
Best for
Handy people

TaskRabbit connects you with people who need help with tasks — moving furniture, assembling IKEA stuff, cleaning, yard work. Rates are solid ($25–$60/hr depending on the task) and you set your own schedule. Takes a few days to get approved, so sign up now even if you don't need cash today.

4. Offer a Skill Online

Fiverr / Freelance Gigs

Per project
$25–$150+
Time to cash
2–7 days
Best skills
Writing, design, video editing

If you can write, design, edit video, do voiceovers, or basically any digital skill, you can make money on Fiverr. Basic gigs start at $25–$50. You won't get rich overnight, but getting your first few clients can realistically happen within a week. Even something like writing product descriptions or creating social media posts can pay.

Don't overthink this: The fastest $100 usually comes from the simplest idea — sell something, mow a lawn, or deliver food. Pick one and do it today instead of researching for a week.

5. Return Stuff You Bought and Don't Need

This sounds obvious but a lot of people forget about it. Go through your recent purchases and check return windows. Clothes with tags still on, gadgets you never opened, subscriptions you forgot about. Most stores have 30–90 day return windows. This isn't making money, technically, but it puts cash back in your pocket just as fast.

The Bottom Line

Making $100 fast is less about finding a secret method and more about taking action on something simple. Selling stuff you own is the fastest zero-effort route. Gig apps are reliable if you have a car. Local services like mowing or pressure washing pay the most per hour.

Pick whichever one fits your situation and do it today. An hour of actual action beats a week of planning every time.

Want to build something more consistent? Check out our guides on starting a pressure washing business or flipping items on Facebook Marketplace for money that keeps coming in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really make $100 in one day?

Yes. Selling unused items, mowing a couple of lawns, or working a busy delivery shift can each realistically produce $100 in a single day — no special skills needed.

What if I don't have a car for delivery apps?

Selling items on Facebook Marketplace and local services like lawn mowing or pressure washing don't require a car, and both can pay out same-day.

How fast do gig apps like DoorDash actually pay?

Most gig apps offer same-day "Fast Pay" cash-outs for a small fee, so you can have your $100 the same day you earn it.
