Best Side Hustles for Beginners With No Money

By MoneyWise · July 6, 2026 · 8 min read
Updated: July 2026

Most side hustle lists are full of ideas that either need a lot of startup money, take months to pay off, or just don't work for most people. This list is different — these are side hustles that real beginners with zero budget can actually start, and actually make money from.

They're ranked loosely by how fast you can earn and how low the barrier to entry is. If you're starting from scratch, start at the top of the list.

1. Selling on Facebook Marketplace (Best for Zero Budget)

Reselling / Flipping

$0 to start Pay same day $500–$3,000/mo potential

You don't need any money to start — just sell what you own. Once you have some cash, you buy cheap items and resell them for profit. People flip clothes, furniture, electronics, tools, and anything else. The skill is knowing what's underpriced and what people actually want to buy. Check out our full guide on what to flip on Marketplace for specifics.

The beauty of flipping is that your profit scales with your effort and knowledge. The more you learn about what sells, the better your margins get.

2. Delivery Driving (Most Consistent Income)

DoorDash / Uber Eats / Instacart

$0 to start Same-day pay $800–$2,000/mo part-time

If you have a car and a clean driving record, you can be delivering within a day or two. These apps pay weekly (or instantly with Fast Pay), so this is the most consistent entry-level side hustle out there. A 15–20 hour week of deliveries can bring in $400–$700 depending on your market and time slots.

Pro tip: Multi-app — run DoorDash and Uber Eats simultaneously. Accept the best offer, reject the rest. Drivers who do this consistently earn 20–30% more per hour than single-app drivers.

3. Freelance Services on Fiverr or Upwork

Writing, Editing, Design, Social Media

$0 to start Pay within 1 week $500–$5,000+/mo

If you can write clearly, create graphics in Canva, edit photos, do voiceovers, or manage a social media account, you have a marketable skill. List a gig on Fiverr starting at $25–$50. It takes time to get your first client, but once the reviews start coming in, orders follow. The ceiling is high — experienced freelancers make thousands per month working from home.

Don't have an obvious skill? These are learnable in a few weeks: basic video editing (CapCut or DaVinci), creating Instagram/Pinterest graphics in Canva, writing product descriptions, or transcription. All are in demand right now.

4. Local Service Business (Highest Hourly Rate)

Lawn Care, Cleaning, Pressure Washing

Low startup cost Cash same day $25–$60/hr

Local services pay the best hourly rate of anything on this list. Lawn mowing pays $30–$75 per yard. House cleaning pays $80–$150 per session. Pressure washing pays $75–$200 per job. You can start with borrowed equipment and reinvest your first few payments into your own tools. Read our pressure washing startup guide if you want to start there — it's one of the fastest businesses to scale.

5. Selling Handmade or Print-on-Demand Items on Etsy

Etsy Shop (Digital or Physical Products)

$0.20/listing to start Passive once set up $200–$2,000+/mo

Etsy is great for selling digital downloads (printable planners, wall art, templates) or physical handmade items. Digital products are the most beginner-friendly — you make something once and sell it forever. A popular digital planner or resume template can make $500+ per month with no ongoing work. With print-on-demand, you design T-shirts, mugs, or totes and a partner service prints and ships them for you.

6. Tutoring or Teaching What You Know

Tutoring / Teaching

$0 to start Pay same week $20–$60/hr

If you're good at any academic subject, a trade skill, or even something like cooking or playing guitar, you can charge people to teach them. Tutoring students in math or English pays $25–$50/hr locally or via apps like Wyzant. Platforms like Skillshare or Teachable let you record lessons once and collect payments passively. Or just post locally on Facebook and offer one-on-one sessions.

The One Thing That Separates Success From Failure

Most people who "try a side hustle and it doesn't work" gave up after a week. The truth is, every single thing on this list works — but all of them require consistency. Delivery drivers who earn $1,500/month show up regularly. Fiverr sellers who make real money invest time in their profile and learn from early reviews. Resellers who flip $2,000/month study their market constantly.

Pick one thing, commit to it for 30 days, and actually measure your results. Then decide if it's worth continuing or pivoting. One month of real effort gives you more information than a year of research.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which side hustle pays the fastest with no money down?

Selling items you already own on Facebook Marketplace is the fastest — there's no startup cost and buyers often pay same-day.

How many hours a week do I need to make this work?

Most people on this list see results with 10–20 hours a week. Delivery driving and local services scale directly with hours worked; freelancing and Etsy pay off more with consistency than raw hours.

Do I need good credit or a business license to start?

No. Every hustle on this list — flipping, delivery apps, freelancing, local services, Etsy, and tutoring — can be started as an individual with no credit check or business registration required to get going.
